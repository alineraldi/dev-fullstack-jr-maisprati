The Variable Trogon (Trogon surrucura), known for its strikingly vibrant plumage, is a bird species native to the forests of South America, particularly in Brazil, Argentina, Paraguay, and Bolivia. Males display a bold contrast of iridescent green or blue on their heads, necks, and backs, with a bright red belly, while females have subtler gray tones. A unique trait of this bird is its habit of staying very still on branches, making it hard to spot despite its vivid colors. Curiously, the Variable Trogon is frugivorous but also consumes insects, displaying remarkable versatility in its diet. Another interesting fact is that trogons nest in tree cavities, often excavated by other species, like woodpeckers. Despite their beauty, their soft, low calls can be surprisingly elusive in dense forests.
